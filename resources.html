Get prepared for severe weather

Extreme weather conditions pose a serious threat to the health and welfare of our communities, and have the potential to lead to illnesses and fatalities. These weather events, such as thunderstorms, hail, blizzards, strong winds, or heavy rainfall, can occur unexpectedly and at any time of the year. An Extreme Heat Emergency is identified by a consistent rise in daytime and nighttime temperatures that exceed the typical seasonal range.

How Climate Change Can Affect Your Mental Health

Climate change can impact mental health, and the CDC recognizes the importance of promoting mental well-being.Extreme weather events and climate change can cause anxiety and post-traumatic stress disorders. However, proactive planning can help communities promote mental health and build resilience. It's important to understand the effects of climate change on mental health and take action to support mental well-being.
